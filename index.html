Cheetah

The cheetah, the fastest land animal, can sprint up to 60 miles per hour. Its slender body and long legs are built for speed, while black tear marks help reduce glare. Native to Africa, cheetahs face threats from habitat loss and human conflict.

Here are some intrsting facts

  1. Cheetahs can reach speeds of 60-70 miles per hour (97-113 km/h) in short bursts.
  2. They can go from 0 to 60 miles per hour in just 3 seconds.
  3. They typically weigh between 77 and 143 pounds (35 to 65 kg).
  4. They have excellent eyesight, which helps them spot prey from a distance.

Cheetahs are true predators in their enviorment here are some of there prey

  • Gazelles especially Thomson's and Grant's gazelles.
  • Impala A common target due to their abundance and size.
  • Springbok another small antelope that they often hunt.
  • Young Wildebeest though not as common, cheetahs may prey on young wildebeest.
